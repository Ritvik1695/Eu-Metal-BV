HOT ROLLED (BLACK & PICKLED) STEEL COILS AND SHEETS (HR/PO)

Hot Rolled Steel is often used in applications where minutely speciﬁc dimensions aren’t crucial. Railroad tracks and construction projects often use hot-rolled steel. Also, these products are used in non-critical surface applications such as automotive frames, wheels, small parts, construction products, household items, agricultural equipments, tubes & sections, oﬀ-highway equipments, guardrails, cylinders, racks & shelves, plates & slabs etc

COLD ROLLED STEEL COILS AND SHEETS

Cold Rolled Steel is used in applications that demand higher surface quality, such as exposed automobile and appliance panels. The unique properties of cold rolled steel are known for being extremely ductile. Cold rolled steel is mainly used for electrical appliances, such as refrigerator, washing machine, freezer, air conditioner, water heater, electric rice cooker, ovens. Also automotive components, bicycle parts, construction & building components, domestic appliances, furniture, round & square tubes and sections, electrical ﬁttings & panels etc.

Galvanisation or galvanising is the process of applying a protective zinc coating to steel or iron, to prevent rusting. The most common method of hot-dip galvanising is submerging in a bath of molten zinc. Galvanised steel is widely used in applications where corrosion resistance is needed without the cost of stainless steel, and is considered superior in terms of cost and life-cycle. ELECTROLYTIC TINPLATE/TIN FREE STEEL/TIN MILL BLACK PLATE COILS

This is a thin sheet of steel with a coating of tin and is also known as packaging steel. By selecting a proper temper grade, appropriate formability is obtained for various applications as well as the required strength after forming. It is specially used for manufacturing of beverage cans, food packaging like edible oil, preserved food, pickles, biscuits, chocolates and non-food applications like decorative items, toys, caps etc.
